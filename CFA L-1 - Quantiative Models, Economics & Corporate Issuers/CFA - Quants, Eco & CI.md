## Quantitative Methods

Quantitative Methods form the analytical foundation of finance. This section introduces mathematical tools used to evaluate financial decisions and interpret data.

### Time Value of Money

The concept of time value of money explains how the value of money changes over time due to interest rates.

$$
PV = \frac{FV}{(1 + r)^n}
$$

For a stream of cash flows:

$$
PV = \sum_{t=1}^{T} \frac{CF_t}{(1 + r)^t}
$$

These formulations are essential for valuing investments, bonds, and capital projects.

---

### Probability and Statistical Modeling

Financial markets involve uncertainty, which is modeled using probability theory.

Expected return:

$$
E[R] = \sum_{i=1}^{n} p_i R_i
$$

Risk (variance):

$$
\sigma^2 = \sum_{i=1}^{n} p_i (R_i - E[R])^2
$$

These metrics are used to quantify expected performance and risk of financial assets.

---

### Regression and Predictive Modeling

Regression models help identify relationships between variables, commonly used in financial forecasting.

$$
Y = \beta_0 + \beta_1 X + \epsilon
$$

This is widely applied in asset pricing models and trend analysis.

## Economics

Economics provides the macro and micro foundations for understanding financial markets and policy impacts.

### Demand and Supply Modeling

Market demand depends on multiple factors:

$$
Q_d = f(P, I, P_s, P_c)
$$

This helps analyze consumer behavior and price sensitivity.

---

### Macroeconomic Framework

The overall economy is modeled using national income identity:

$$
GDP = C + I + G + (X - M)
$$

This equation captures economic activity and is central to macroeconomic analysis.

---

### Elasticity

Elasticity measures responsiveness of demand:

$$
E_d = \frac{\% \Delta Q}{\% \Delta P}
$$

It is critical for pricing strategies and market behavior analysis.

## Corporate Issuers

This section focuses on financial decision-making within firms using valuation and optimization models.

### Investment Decision (NPV)

Net Present Value is used to evaluate projects:

$$
NPV = \sum_{t=0}^{T} \frac{CF_t}{(1 + r)^t}
$$

Projects with positive NPV add value to the firm.

---

### Cost of Capital

The weighted average cost of capital represents the firm’s overall cost of financing:

$$
WACC = \frac{E}{V} R_e + \frac{D}{V} R_d (1 - T)
$$

This is used as a discount rate in investment decisions.

---

### Capital Structure

Firm value under leverage:

$$
V_L = V_U + T_c D
$$

This model explains how debt impacts firm valuation.
