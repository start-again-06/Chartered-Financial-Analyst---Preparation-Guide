## Overview

This repository presents a comprehensive, structured, and concept-driven set of notes for the Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA) Level 1 curriculum, specifically focusing on Financial Statement Analysis and Equity Investments.

The objective of this material is not just to summarize concepts, but to build a strong analytical foundation by integrating accounting principles, financial reasoning, and quantitative models. ## Financial Statement Analysis

Financial Statement Analysis involves evaluating a firm’s financial health, performance, and future prospects using its reported financial data. It serves as the foundation for equity valuation, credit analysis, and investment decision-making.

---

### Financial Statement Analysis Framework

A systematic six-step process ensures consistency and depth in analysis:

1. **Define Objective and Context** – Identify the purpose of analysis  
2. **Collect Data** – Financial statements, industry data, and macro factors  
3. **Process Data** – Adjust statements, compute ratios, normalize figures  
4. **Analyze and Interpret** – Identify trends, risks, and opportunities  
5. **Report Conclusions** – Communicate insights effectively  
6. **Update Continuously** – Revise analysis with new information  

This structured approach ensures that analysis remains objective, repeatable, and decision-oriented.

---

### Income Statement Analysis

The income statement reflects a firm’s operational performance over a period.

#### Revenue Recognition

Revenue is recognized when economic value is delivered, not necessarily when cash is received. The standard framework follows:

1. Identify contract  
2. Identify performance obligations  
3. Determine transaction price  
4. Allocate price  
5. Recognize revenue  

This ensures that revenue reflects true economic activity rather than timing distortions.

---

#### Expense Recognition

Expenses are recorded based on their relationship with revenue:

- **Matching Principle** → Align expenses with related revenue  
- **Capitalization** → Spread cost over useful life  
- **Expensing** → Immediate recognition  

Impact on financials:

- Capitalizing increases short-term profitability but defers costs  
- Expensing reduces current earnings but improves transparency  

---

### Earnings Per Share (EPS)

EPS measures profitability available to equity shareholders.

Basic EPS:

$$
\text{EPS} = \frac{\text{Net Income} - \text{Preferred Dividends}}{\text{Weighted Avg Shares}}
$$

Diluted EPS accounts for potential dilution from convertible instruments:

$$
\text{Diluted EPS} = \frac{\text{Adjusted Income}}{\text{Total Diluted Shares}}
$$

This distinction is critical for understanding true shareholder value.

---

### Balance Sheet Analysis

The balance sheet provides a snapshot of financial position, capturing assets, liabilities, and equity.

#### Goodwill and Intangibles

$$
\text{Goodwill} = \text{Purchase Price} - \text{Fair Value of Net Assets}
$$

- Represents acquisition premium  
- Not amortized but tested for impairment  
- Sensitive indicator of overpayment in acquisitions  

---

### Financial Ratios

Ratios convert raw financial data into meaningful insights.

#### Liquidity Ratios

$$
\text{Current Ratio} = \frac{CA}{CL}
$$

$$
\text{Quick Ratio} = \frac{Cash + AR}{CL}
$$

Measure short-term solvency and ability to meet obligations.

---

#### Solvency Ratios

$$
\text{Debt-to-Equity} = \frac{\text{Total Debt}}{\text{Equity}}
$$

Indicates financial leverage and long-term risk.

---

#### Profitability Ratios

$$
\text{ROE} = \frac{\text{Net Income}}{\text{Equity}}
$$

Captures efficiency in generating returns for shareholders.

---

### Cash Flow Analysis

Cash flows provide a clearer picture of financial reality than accrual accounting.

Core identity:

$$
CFO + CFI + CFF = \Delta Cash
$$

Indirect method:

$$
CFO = NI + \text{Non-cash Charges} - \Delta WC
$$

A key analytical insight:
- If **Net Income > CFO consistently → potential earnings quality issues**

---

### Free Cash Flow

Free cash flow represents cash available for investors.

$$
FCFF = CFO + \text{Interest}(1 - t) - \text{CapEx}
$$

$$
FCFE = CFO - \text{CapEx} + \text{Net Borrowing}
$$

These metrics are essential for valuation and capital allocation decisions.

---

### Inventory Analysis

Inventory management reflects operational efficiency and demand conditions.

$$
\text{Inventory Turnover} = \frac{COGS}{\text{Average Inventory}}
$$

$$
DOH = \frac{365}{\text{Inventory Turnover}}
$$

- Higher turnover → efficient operations  
- Rising inventory → potential demand slowdown  

---

### DuPont Analysis

Breaks down ROE into fundamental drivers:

$$
ROE = \text{Net Margin} \times \text{Asset Turnover} \times \text{Financial Leverage}
$$

This decomposition helps identify whether returns are driven by:
- Profitability  
- Efficiency  
- Leverage  

---

### Financial Reporting Quality

Financial statements vary in reliability:

1. High-quality, sustainable earnings  
2. Conservative accounting  
3. Aggressive accounting  
4. Earnings management  
5. Non-compliance  
6. Fraud  

Understanding this spectrum is critical for avoiding misleading conclusions.

## Equity Investments

Equity Investments focus on how markets function and how securities are valued based on expected future cash flows.

---

### Market Organization

Financial markets perform three core roles:

- Efficient allocation of capital  
- Facilitation of trading and liquidity  
- Price discovery through information  

---

### Market Indexes

Indexes measure market performance and serve as benchmarks.

$$
\text{Return} = \frac{V_1 - V_0}{V_0}
$$

Different weighting methods (price, equal, market-cap) affect index behavior and interpretation.

---

### Market Efficiency

Markets are classified based on information incorporation:

- **Weak-form** → Past data reflected  
- **Semi-strong** → Public information reflected  
- **Strong-form** → All information reflected  

Efficiency determines whether active strategies can outperform.

---

### Equity Securities

Types of equity instruments:

- Common stock → Residual ownership  
- Preferred stock → Fixed income-like features  
- Convertible securities → Hybrid instruments  

Each carries distinct risk-return characteristics.

---

### Company Analysis

Firm performance is driven by operational economics:

$$
\text{Operating Profit} = Q(P - VC) - FC
$$

Where profitability depends on pricing power, cost structure, and scale.

---

### Industry Analysis

Competitive dynamics are analyzed using Porter’s Five Forces:

1. Competitive rivalry  
2. Threat of new entrants  
3. Threat of substitutes  
4. Buyer power  
5. Supplier power  

This framework determines long-term profitability potential.

---

### Forecasting

Forecasting translates analysis into forward-looking estimates.

Revenue model:

$$
\text{Revenue} = \text{Price} \times \text{Quantity}
$$

Working capital estimation:

$$
AR = DSO \times \frac{\text{Revenue}}{365}
$$

$$
Inventory = DOH \times \frac{COGS}{365}
$$

These relationships form the backbone of financial modeling.

---

## Equity Valuation

Valuation converts expectations into intrinsic value.

---

### Dividend Discount Model (DDM)

$$
V_0 = \sum_{t=1}^{\infty} \frac{D_t}{(1 + k)^t}
$$

---

### Gordon Growth Model

$$
V_0 = \frac{D_1}{k - g}
$$

A simplified model assuming constant growth.

---

### Growth Rate

$$
g = ROE \times (1 - \text{Payout Ratio})
$$

Links profitability with reinvestment policy.

---

### Relative Valuation

Widely used in practice:

$$
P/E = \frac{\text{Price}}{\text{EPS}}
$$

$$
EV = \text{Market Cap} + \text{Debt} - \text{Cash}
$$

$$
EV/EBITDA = \frac{EV}{EBITDA}
$$

These multiples enable comparison across firms and industries.
